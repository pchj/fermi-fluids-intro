What is a Fermi Fluid?

A Fermi fluid (or Fermi liquid) is a theoretical model that describes the behavior of interacting fermions at low temperatures. Despite strong interactions between particles, the system behaves remarkably like a gas of non-interacting fermions with renormalized parameters.

The key insight of Landau's Fermi fluid theory is that low-energy excitations can be described as "quasiparticles" that have a one-to-one correspondence with the particles of a non-interacting Fermi gas.

The Fermi Sphere and Fermi Surface

In momentum space, fermions at T = 0 fill all states up to the Fermi momentum \(k_F\), forming a sphere in 3D. The boundary of this filled region is called the Fermi surface.

The Fermi energy is given by: $$E_F = \frac{\hbar^2 k_F^2}{2m}$$

Quasiparticles and Landau Parameters

Quasiparticles are the elementary excitations of the Fermi fluid. They have:

  • Renormalized mass: \(m^* = m(1 + F_1^s/3)\)
  • Finite lifetime: \(\tau \propto (E - E_F)^{-2}\)
  • Modified interactions: Described by Landau parameters \(F_l^{s,a}\)

The quasiparticle energy near the Fermi surface is: $$E(\mathbf{k}) = E_F + v_F^*(|\mathbf{k}| - k_F)$$

Interaction Effects

Interactions between quasiparticles are characterized by the Landau interaction function: $$f(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}') = \sum_{l=0}^{\infty} \left[F_l^s P_l(\cos\theta) + F_l^a P_l(\cos\theta) \boldsymbol{\sigma} \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}'\right]$$

Key consequences:

  • Compressibility: \(\kappa = \kappa_0/(1 + F_0^s)\)
  • Spin susceptibility: \(\chi = \chi_0/(1 + F_0^a)\)
  • Specific heat: \(C_V \propto m^* T\)

Transport Properties

Fermi fluids exhibit characteristic temperature dependencies in transport:

  • Resistivity: \(\rho(T) = \rho_0 + AT^2\) (TÂ²-law)
  • Heat capacity: \(C_V = \gamma T\) (linear in T)
  • Wilson ratio: \(\mathcal{R} = \frac{\pi^2 k_B^2 \chi}{3 \mu_B^2 \gamma}\)

These relationships break down when Fermi fluid theory fails, signaling exotic quantum phases.
